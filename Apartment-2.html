Bills bloom across the table like dull paper flowers; the sink holds a leaning city of dishes. Cans clatter—lager, stout, anything on sale—and the air sweetens with stale malt. The man speaks softly some days, telling stories to the glass and the cat alike. On others, he smells like pickles soaked in liquor. He became aggressive with repulsive looks. Should the kitten hide under couch or rub his ankle with hopeful whiskers?
