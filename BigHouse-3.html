“Not safe for the baby,” someone repeats, and the car smells like leather and lemon. The cattery is bright and metallic; intake forms, a numbered tag, and a cage that clicks shut. Bowls are shared, and tuna becomes a smear to guard while a striped tom shoulders the kitten aside. A timid calico watches from a shelf, blinking slowly like a frayed little lamp. The litter is never quite fresh, and nights hum like fluorescent dusk. Kitten falls asleep here.
