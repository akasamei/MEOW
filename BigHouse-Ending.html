The kitten stays, and a visitor arrives in a blue cardigan that smells like lavender. “This one,” she murmurs, lifting kitty gently, slipping a blanket into a soft carrier. Home is narrow but bright: boiled eggs, chicken broth, and a sunny square on a rug. Years pass in teacup clinks and evening radio hush. Life is modest but loving; the cat dies a natural death, held and at peace.
